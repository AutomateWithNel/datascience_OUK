## Case: OUK football club Case

The Open University of Kenya (OUK) is a member of the national football competition (NFC). The NFC is made of several teams, some of which come from other Universities and others come from outside. Just like the OUK team, the other teams have names, a technical team led by a coach, a captain (who is also a player) and a number of players. Each team belongs to an institution (e.g. OUK football Club belongs to OUK). According to the football federation rules, a player can only belong to one team. As usual, a player has a name, skill classification (e.g. best, average). A player can also have a set of fault records which have attracted yellow or red cards.  Whenever two teams are playing, there is a host and a guest team. The date and time for each game is recorded alongside the scores for each team.
